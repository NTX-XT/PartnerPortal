## Activity 1.0: Build Partner Delivery Capacity
**Lane:** Channel/Partner Team

### Tasks:
• Partner Identification & Recruitment
  - Target partners with existing Nintex relationships
  - Assess: K2 background, PS capability, strategic alignment
  - Recruitment rate: 3-5 partners per quarter

• K2 Masterclass Training Delivery
  - 2-day intensive virtual training
  - Modules: K2 overview, migration methodology, Nintex Cloud architecture, hands-on labs
  - Post-training: Access to migration toolkit and resources

• Certification Journey Execution
  - Associate Tier: K2 Masterclass + knowledge assessment → Shadow deliveries (3-6 months)
  - Professional Tier: 2 supervised migrations → Lead 40-120 hour engagements independently (6-12 months)
  - Expert Tier: 5 successful migrations → Complex 200+ hour engagements, mentoring (12+ months)
  - Quality gates: ≥4.0 CSAT score, on-time delivery required for advancement

• Partner Capabilities Dashboard Maintenance
  - Track: Certification tier, capacity availability, active engagements, CSAT scores, Net New ARR contribution
  - Update: Weekly for active partners, monthly for idle
  - Access: PS Overlay, Channel Team, Sales leadership

### Notes:
**Q: How many partners should we certify?**
A: Target 10-15 certified partners (minimum 5 Professional tier) to ensure adequate capacity coverage while maintaining quality control. Maintain 20% overcapacity buffer.

**Q: What if a partner fails certification?**
A: Remediation path available: Additional training, extended mentorship. Maximum 2 re-certification attempts before program exit.

**Q: What support during certification?**
A: Dedicated partner success manager, access to technical experts, migration toolkit, practice environments, co-delivery opportunities.

### Deliverables:
• Certified partner roster by tier
• Partner Capabilities Dashboard (live)
• Certification journey documentation
• Partner agreements executed

### Success Metrics:
• Number of certified partners by tier
• Time-to-certification by partner
• Certification pass rate
• Partner satisfaction with enablement program

## Activity 2.0: Qualify Migration Opportunity
**Lane:** Sales (AM/AD), PS Overlay

### Tasks:
• Opportunity Identification
  - Monitor: SPOP licenses approaching renewal (90-120 days), cloud adoption initiatives, K2 pain points
  - Sales stages: Focus on Stage 1 (Qualified) and Stage 2 (Discovery)
  - Types: Proactive (Nintex-initiated) or Reactive (customer-requested)

• Deployment Service Model Assessment
  - Self-Deployed: Customer has capability, simple workflows, prefers DIY
  - Nintex PS-Led: Complex (200+ hours), strategic account, unique requirements
  - Partner-Led: Standard to moderate (40-200 hours), customer open to partner, capacity constraints

• PS Overlay Intake Submission
  - Required fields: Customer info, SPOP environment details (workflow count, complexity, integrations), technical maturity, timeline, budget
  - Timing: During Stage 2 (Discovery/Qualification)
  - SLA: PS Overlay reviews within 2 business days

• Customer Needs & Complexity Assessment
  - Technical factors: Workflow count (0-10 simple, 11-50 moderate, 51+ complex), integrations, custom code, data migration
  - Customer factors: Technical skill level, availability, change management needs, timeline flexibility
  - Service hour estimation: Simple 40-80h, Moderate 80-160h, Complex 160-300+h
  - Output: Complexity rating (1-5 scale) and recommended delivery model

### Notes:
**Q: When should we engage PS Overlay in the sales cycle?**
A: During Stage 2 (Discovery/Qualification) - early enough to shape solution, late enough to ensure genuine opportunity.

**Q: What if customer initially wants self-serve but PS Overlay identifies complexity?**
A: Sales presents risk assessment and recommendation, customer makes informed decision. Document customer choice if proceeding against recommendation.

**Q: How do we handle borderline complexity cases?**
A: Default to partner delivery with PS Overlay safety net (checkpoint reviews at 25%, 50%, 75% completion).

**Q: What if no suitable partner is available?**
A: Options: (1) Adjust timeline to match partner availability, (2) Escalate to Nintex PS capacity, (3) Customer self-serve with premium support.

### Deliverables:
• Completed PS Overlay intake form
• Complexity assessment (1-5 score)
• Service hour estimate
• Delivery model recommendation

### Success Metrics:
• Opportunity qualification time (intake to decision)
• Accuracy of complexity assessment vs actual delivery
• Delivery model recommendation acceptance rate

## Decision Gate: Delivery Model Decision
**Lane:** PS Overlay

### Decision Criteria:
**Route to Nintex PS if:**
• Complexity score ≥ 4 (highly complex)
• Strategic account (Enterprise/Global tier)
• Estimated hours > 200
• Customer specifically requests Nintex-direct
• Partner capacity unavailable in required geography/timeframe
• New technology/methodology pilot

**Route to Partner Delivery if:**
• Complexity score ≤ 3 (standard to moderate)
• Estimated hours 40-200
• Customer open to partner engagement
• Certified partner available with appropriate tier
• Opportunity for Net New ARR partner contribution
• Geographic/timezone alignment beneficial

**Route to Self-Serve if:**
• Complexity score 1-2 (simple)
• Customer has technical capability and preference
• Estimated hours < 40
• Customer budget constraints

## Activity 3.0: Select Partner & Allocate Opportunity
**Lane:** Channel/Partner Team, PS Overlay

### Tasks:
• Round Robin Allocation Application
  - Filter partners by: Certification tier required (based on complexity), geographic coverage, current capacity, technical specialization
  - Queue mechanics: Eligible partners rotate, next available gets offer, 24-48h to accept/decline
  - Exceptions: Customer-requested partner, strategic account, specialized technical requirements

• Multi-Factor Partner Evaluation (Weighted Scoring)
  - **Factor 1: Certification Tier & Capability Match (30% weight)**
    * Complexity 1-2: Associate or Professional
    * Complexity 3: Professional required
    * Complexity 4-5: Professional or Expert required
  
  - **Factor 2: Current Capacity/Availability (25% weight)**
    * Formula: Available hours ÷ Estimated hours ≥ 1.2x (20% buffer)
    * Consider current active engagements
  
  - **Factor 3: Customer Preference (10% weight)**
    * Existing relationship: +10 points
    * Customer specifically requests: Priority allocation
    * Geographic/cultural alignment: +5 points
  
  - **Factor 4: Strategic Value & Net New ARR (10% weight)**
    * Trailing 12-month ARR: <$50K Standard, $50K-$150K High-value, >$150K Strategic
  
  - **Factor 5: Historical Quality & CSAT (25% weight)**
    * CSAT: ≥4.5 Excellent (priority), 4.0-4.49 Good (standard), 3.5-3.99 Acceptable (monitored), <3.5 PIP required
    * On-time delivery: ≥90% Excellent, 80-89% Good, 70-79% Acceptable, <70% Concern
  
  - Minimum qualifying score: 70/100

• Channel Team Partner Recommendation
  - Package includes: Partner profile, allocation rationale (scoring), capacity commitment, commercial terms, risk assessment
  - Submitted to PS Overlay for approval
  - SLA: 2 business days

• PS Overlay Approval
  - Review: Capability match, quality history, capacity sufficiency, commercial terms, risk assessment
  - Decision: Approved, Approved with Conditions, Not Approved
  - SLA: 1 business day

• Customer-Partner Introduction (Sales-led)
  - Introduction call: Partner background, Nintex certification, proposed approach, commercial terms, Nintex oversight
  - Meeting agenda: Partner credentials, customer requirements, timeline planning, expectation setting
  - Customer acceptance checkpoint required before proceeding

### Notes:
**Q: What if the top-scoring partner declines the opportunity?**
A: Move to next-highest scoring partner. After 3 declines, reassess opportunity requirements or timeline.

**Q: How do we balance Round Robin fairness with performance-based allocation?**
A: High-performing partners (≥4.5 CSAT) get preferential allocation within rotation; low-performing partners (<4.0) require approval for each allocation.

**Q: What if customer doesn't want the assigned partner?**
A: Sales works with customer to understand concerns; if valid, provide alternative. Document reason for rotation adjustment.

**Q: How do we prevent partner cherry-picking?**
A: Pattern of excessive declines impacts partner standing; maintain decline rate metric (<20% acceptable).

### Deliverables:
• Partner allocation recommendation with scoring
• PS Overlay approval documentation
• Customer-partner introduction scheduled
• Engagement kickoff authorization

### Success Metrics:
• Partner allocation time (opportunity received to partner assigned)
• Partner acceptance rate
• Customer acceptance of partner assignment
• Allocation fairness (opportunities per partner over time)

## Activity 4.0: Structure Commercial Terms
**Lane:** Sales (AM/AD), Channel/Partner Team, Finance/Deal Desk

### Tasks:
• Deal Source Classification
  - **Nintex-Sourced:** Customer identified by Nintex Sales, standard partner margin, Nintex maintains customer relationship
  - **Partner-Sourced:** Partner brings opportunity, enhanced margin (referral bonus), partner maintains relationship

• ARR Uplift & Service Hours Investment Calculation
  - Formula: New Cloud ARR - Current SPOP ARR = Net ARR Uplift
  - **Tier 1 Investment (≥$25K uplift):** Nintex contributes up to 50% service cost (max $15K), customer pays 50%+
  - **Tier 2 Investment ($10K-$24.9K):** Nintex contributes up to 30% (max $8K), customer pays 70%+
  - **Tier 3 Investment (<$10K):** Nintex contributes 0-10%, customer pays 90-100%+
  - Strategic exceptions: Enterprise accounts, market entry, competitive displacement (requires Sales + Finance approval)

• Partner Margin Structure & Payment Terms
  - **Associate Tier:** Nintex bills $175-$200/h, Partner receives $120-$140/h (70-75% margin)
  - **Professional Tier:** Nintex bills $200-$225/h, Partner receives $150-$170/h (75% margin)
  - **Expert Tier:** Nintex bills $225-$250/h, Partner receives $175-$195/h (75-78% margin)
  - Enhanced margin: Partner-sourced +5-10%, High-volume (5+ engagements/year) +5%, Excellence (≥4.7 CSAT) +$10/h
  - Payment terms: Net 30 from customer payment receipt, or milestone-based (40% kickoff, 40% at 50%, 20% completion)

• Risk Mitigation & Quality Assurance Provisions
  - **NTE (Not-to-Exceed) caps:** 10-15% buffer above estimate, overages require change order
  - **Performance holdbacks:** 10-20% held for new/Associate partners until customer acceptance and ≥3.5 CSAT (removed after 3 successful deliveries)
  - **Quality checkpoints:** Kickoff, 25% (Associates), 50% (all), Pre-delivery, Post-delivery retrospective (all mandatory)
  - **Warranty:** 30-day post-go-live, partner remediates defects at no cost, 24h response for critical issues

• Deal Documentation & Approvals
  - Required: SOW (scope, deliverables, timeline), Partner Service Agreement, Customer Service Agreement, Engagement Plan
  - Approval chain: Sales (deal structure), Channel (partner terms), PS Overlay (scope/quality), Finance (exceptions), Legal (non-standard terms)
  - Deal Desk submission and validation (SLA: 2 business days)

• Contract Execution & Kickoff Authorization
  - Customer and partner contracts fully executed
  - Payment secured (PO received or credit approved)
  - Partner resources allocated and confirmed
  - PS Overlay grants "green light" for engagement start
  - Kickoff meeting scheduled within 5 business days

### Notes:
**Q: What if customer budget cannot cover full service cost after Nintex contribution?**
A: Options: (1) Reduce scope to fit budget, (2) Phased approach with priority workflows first, (3) Customer self-serve with partner support, (4) Escalate for strategic investment.

**Q: How do we handle partner requests for higher margins?**
A: Review based on performance history, market rates, complexity, strategic value. Exceptions require Sales leadership approval.

**Q: How do we ensure partner doesn't underbid to win allocation?**
A: PS Overlay validates hour estimates against benchmarks; history of underestimating impacts future allocation decisions.

**Q: What happens if customer doesn't pay?**
A: Nintex collections process with customer; partner payment not contingent on customer payment (Nintex bears credit risk).

### Deliverables:
• Fully executed customer service agreement
• Fully executed partner service agreement
• Statement of Work (SOW)
• Engagement plan with timeline
• Financial terms documented in CRM
• Kickoff authorization from PS Overlay

### Success Metrics:
• Deal cycle time (allocation to contract execution)
• Partner margin satisfaction
• Customer perceived value vs cost
• Commercial terms exception rate

## Activity 5.0: Execute Partner-Led Migration Delivery
**Lane:** Partner Delivery Team, PS Overlay, Customer

### Overview:
Partner-led technical delivery with Nintex quality oversight ensuring customer success.

**DELIVERY LIFECYCLE:** Kickoff → Discovery → Build → Testing → Deployment → Hypercare

**PARALLEL ACTIVITIES (occur simultaneously):**
• Partner executes migration delivery
• PS Overlay monitors and provides oversight
• Customer collaborates and validates
• Weekly status reporting to all stakeholders

### Detailed Tasks:

**1. ENGAGEMENT KICKOFF (All parties, Required)**
• Attendees: Partner delivery team, Customer sponsor/technical team, PS Overlay, Sales, Channel Manager
• Agenda: Introductions, scope review, timeline/milestones, communication plan (weekly reports, bi-weekly steering for 120+h engagements), environment access, success criteria, risk identification
• Deliverables: Signed project charter, detailed project plan (Gantt), RACI matrix, communication plan, risk register

**2. DISCOVERY & PLANNING PHASE (Partner-led, Customer collaboration)**
• Current State Assessment: Complete workflow inventory, complexity analysis, custom code documentation, integrations, data models, stakeholder interviews
• Future State Design: Map SPOP/K2 to Nintex Cloud equivalents (direct migrations vs redesigns vs retirements), new architecture, workflow modernization opportunities
• Migration Sequencing: Prioritize by criticality/complexity/dependencies, define waves/phases (Phase 1: simple workflows, Phase 2: business-critical, Phase 3: complex)
• Testing Strategy: Unit testing, integration testing, UAT, parallel run planning, cutover and rollback plans
• Deliverable: Discovery report, future state architecture, migration plan with phasing, risk assessment (customer approval required)

**3. BUILD & CONFIGURATION PHASE (Partner execution)**
• Workflow Development: Build in Nintex Cloud following best practices, document custom code, implement error handling, build for scalability
• Integration Development: REST APIs, database connections, third-party connectors, authentication/security, test integrations independently
• Data Migration: Migrate workflow history (if needed), active instances, metadata, validate data integrity
• Unit Testing: Happy path, error conditions, boundary cases, performance testing, document results, remediate defects

**4. TESTING & VALIDATION PHASE (Partner + Customer)**
• Integration Testing: End-to-end process flows, cross-workflow dependencies, data flow, system integration points
• User Acceptance Testing (UAT): Customer tests real business scenarios by role, full process validation, usability feedback. Partner supports with training and issue triage
• Performance Testing (if applicable): Load testing, stress testing, performance tuning for high-volume workflows
• UAT acceptance criteria must be met before deployment

**5. DEPLOYMENT & GO-LIVE (Partner execution, PS Oversight)**
• Pre-Deployment Checklist: All testing complete, UAT sign-off, production configured, training completed, support plan activated, rollback ready, user communication sent
• Deployment Execution: Migrate to production during maintenance window, follow change control, validate deployment, cutover from legacy (disable SPOP/K2, redirect users)
• Hypercare Period (2-4 weeks): Partner provides elevated support with daily monitoring, rapid response, user support, performance monitoring. PS Overlay monitors daily
• Deliverables: Production deployment confirmation, as-built documentation, admin guides, end user guides, training materials, support handover

**6. QUALITY OVERSIGHT CHECKPOINTS (PS Overlay - Required)**
• Checkpoint 1: Kickoff (All partners) - Validate project plan, scope clarity, team readiness, governance
• Checkpoint 2: Discovery Complete (All partners) - Review documentation, migration approach, risks, customer sign-off
• Checkpoint 3: 25% Complete (Associate partners, Optional others) - Progress, quality, issues, Associate partners get hands-on mentoring
• Checkpoint 4: 50% Complete (All partners) - Milestone achievement, build quality, timeline/budget tracking, relationship health
• Checkpoint 5: Pre-Deployment (All partners) - Testing completion, UAT sign-off, deployment readiness, go-live plan
• Checkpoint 6: Post-Deployment Retrospective (All partners) - Delivery review, lessons learned, CSAT review, partner performance assessment

**7. STATUS REPORTING & COMMUNICATION (Partner responsibility)**
• Weekly Status Reports (every Friday): Progress summary, milestone tracking, risks/issues log, decisions needed, resource/schedule status, budget/hours tracking
• Escalation Procedures:
  - Level 1 (Partner → PS Overlay, 1 business day SLA): Technical challenges, customer concerns, scope creep, timeline/budget risks
  - Level 2 (PS Overlay → Leadership, 4 hour SLA): Customer satisfaction concerns, delivery quality issues, delays >2 weeks, budget overruns >15%
  - Level 3 (Executive, Immediate): Customer at-risk of churn, partner delivery failure, legal/compliance issues

### Notes:
**Q: What if partner falls behind schedule during delivery?**
A: Partner submits recovery plan to PS Overlay. If recovery not feasible: Add partner resources (partner absorbs cost), extend timeline (customer approval), reduce scope (customer approval), or PS Overlay co-delivers.

**Q: What if customer keeps requesting scope changes?**
A: Partner documents all changes, submits to PS Overlay and Sales for customer approval and change order (additional cost/time). Emphasize change management discipline.

**Q: How do we handle conflicts between partner and customer?**
A: PS Overlay serves as neutral mediator. Focus on facts, contract terms, customer success. Escalate to Sales and Partner leadership if needed.

**Q: What if partner identifies technical challenges they cannot solve?**
A: Partner escalates to PS Overlay immediately. PS Overlay provides guidance or assigns Nintex PS expert (partner-funded or Nintex-invested based on situation).

**Q: What happens if workflows don't work properly after go-live?**
A: Partner's 30-day warranty covers defects. Partner must remediate at no cost. Critical issues: 24h response SLA. If partner unable, Nintex steps in with cost recovery.

### Deliverables:
• Completed workflows in Nintex Cloud production
• As-built documentation
• Administrator and technical guides
• End user guides and training materials
• Knowledge transfer completion
• Customer acceptance sign-off
• Weekly status reports
• Checkpoint approval documentation

### Success Metrics:
• On-time delivery rate (within original timeline)
• On-budget delivery rate (within NTE cap)
• Customer satisfaction (CSAT ≥4.0 target)
• Defect rate (post-go-live issues)
• Partner checkpoint pass rate

## Activity 6.0: Evaluate Delivery Success & Partner Performance
**Lane:** PS Overlay, Channel/Partner Team

### Tasks:

• **Customer Acceptance & Sign-Off**
  - Partner requests sign-off when: All deliverables complete, UAT passed, no critical/high defects, documentation delivered, training completed
  - Customer validates against SOW acceptance criteria
  - Gap resolution: Partner addresses deficiencies before final acceptance
  - Deliverables: Workflows in production, as-built docs, admin guides, user guides, knowledge transfer completed, support handover package

• **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) Survey**
  - Administered by PS Overlay (not partner, for objectivity) within 1 week of completion
  - Survey content: Overall satisfaction (1-5 scale), dimension ratings (technical expertise, communication, professionalism, quality, timeline, budget, knowledge transfer, would recommend Y/N)
  - Open-ended: What partner did well, what to improve, additional comments
  - Score interpretation:
    * 4.5-5.0 Excellent: Partner exceeds expectations
    * 4.0-4.49 Good: Partner meets expectations
    * 3.5-3.99 Acceptable: Meets minimum standards, improvement needed
    * 3.0-3.49 Below Acceptable: Performance improvement plan required
    * <3.0 Unacceptable: Partner allocation suspension, remediation required
  - Follow-up: For <4.0, PS Overlay and Partner Manager interview customer. For ≥4.5, request testimonial/reference

• **Partner Performance Evaluation (PS Overlay Assessment)**
  - Deliverable quality: Workflows meet standards, documentation completeness, code quality
  - Process adherence: Checkpoint attendance/preparation, status reporting, escalation usage, change management
  - Project management: Timeline/budget management, risk/issue management, stakeholder communication
  - Technical capability: Problem-solving, platform knowledge, best practices, innovation
  - Performance Rating: Exceeds (5), Meets (4), Partially Meets (3), Below (2), Unacceptable (1)
  - Performance Scorecard: CSAT 40%, PS Overlay 30%, On-time 15%, On-budget 15%

• **Partner Feedback & Coaching Session (Within 2 weeks of completion)**
  - Partner Manager + PS Overlay review scorecard with partner
  - Specific examples: Strengths to leverage, areas for improvement with recommendations
  - Discuss customer feedback (CSAT results and comments)
  - High-performing partners: More complex engagements, Expert tier consideration, mentoring opportunities, marketing co-investment
  - Partners needing improvement: Development plan with timelines, additional training/mentoring, shadowing opportunities
  - Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) for <3.0: 30-60 day plan, specific targets, checkpoints, allocation restrictions (Associate-level only with enhanced oversight), outcomes (successful/extended PIP/program exit)

• **Value Realization & Customer Adoption (30-60 days post-go-live)**
  - Customer Success team conducts: Workflow usage monitoring, performance validation, user adoption assessment, identify lingering issues
  - Outcomes: Green (successful adoption, warranty complete), Yellow (adoption challenges, provide support), Red (significant issues, escalate)
  - Value metrics: Workflow completion rate, user adoption rate, efficiency improvements, business value feedback
  - Identify expansion opportunities: Additional workflows, cross-sell, partner role in future phases

• **Program Continuous Improvement (PS Overlay facilitates)**
  - Post-Delivery Retrospective: Partner, PS Overlay, Sales, Partner Manager attend
  - Questions: What went well? What didn't? What surprised us? What would we do differently?
  - Document lessons learned, identify process/program improvements
  - Program-level insights: Aggregate data across all deliveries (average CSAT, common challenges, best practices, capability gaps)
  - Quarterly program review: Performance trends, effectiveness metrics, curriculum updates, process refinements
  - Share insights with partner community (anonymized): Common pitfalls, success strategies, platform tips

### Notes:
**Q: What if customer provides low CSAT but partner delivered per SOW?**
A: Investigate root cause. May be expectation mismatch, communication gap, or customer unreasonableness. Use as learning for future scoping and expectation setting.

**Q: What if partner disputes the performance evaluation?**
A: Partner Manager reviews with PS Overlay and provides objective evidence. Partner can submit rebuttal. If unresolved, escalate to Partner Program leadership.

**Q: How do we balance being supportive vs maintaining quality standards?**
A: Clear, consistent standards with transparent evaluation. Provide development support but enforce consequences for repeated performance issues.

**Q: What happens to high-performing partners?**
A: Priority allocation for desirable opportunities, faster certification advancement, marketing co-investment (case studies, events), strategic partner consideration.

**Q: What if customer refuses to provide CSAT?**
A: Sales and PS Overlay engage to encourage feedback. If still declines, use PS Overlay assessment only and note non-response (not held against partner).

### Deliverables:
• Customer acceptance documentation
• CSAT survey results
• Partner performance scorecard
• Partner feedback session notes
• Performance Improvement Plan (if needed)
• Post-delivery retrospective report
• Lessons learned documentation
• Program improvement recommendations

### Success Metrics:
• Customer CSAT score (target ≥4.0 program average)
• Partner performance rating distribution
• Customer adoption rate (workflows actively used)
• Post-deployment issue rate
• Partner certification advancement rate
• Program Net Promoter Score

## Decision Gate: Performance Assessment
**Lane:** PS Overlay

### Decision Criteria:

**HIGH PERFORMER (CSAT ≥4.5, Overall Score ≥4.5):**
→ Continue to Activity 7.0 with priority treatment
• Priority allocation for future opportunities
• Fast-track to higher certification tier
• Marketing co-investment opportunities
• Strategic partner consideration

**MEETS EXPECTATIONS (CSAT 4.0-4.49, Overall Score 4.0-4.49):**
→ Continue to Activity 7.0 with standard treatment
• Standard allocation process
• Ongoing enablement and support
• Certification progression on normal timeline

**NEEDS IMPROVEMENT (CSAT 3.5-3.99, Overall Score 3.0-3.99):**
→ Loop back to Activity 3.0 with development plan
• Additional training and mentoring
• Monitored allocation (enhanced oversight)
• Development plan with specific targets
• Re-evaluate after next engagement

**PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (CSAT <3.5, Overall Score <3.0):**
→ Loop back to Activity 1.0 (Rebuild capability)
• 30-60 day formal PIP
• Allocation suspension or restriction
• May require re-certification
• Exit from program if insufficient improvement

## Activity 7.0: Update Partner Capabilities & Evolve Program
**Lane:** Channel/Partner Team, PS Overlay

### Tasks:

• **Ongoing Partner Training & Enablement**
  - Platform Updates (Quarterly): New Nintex features, platform updates, updated best practices, new integrations (virtual webinars, hands-on labs)
  - Advanced Technical Workshops (Bi-annually): Deep-dives on complex topics (advanced patterns, performance optimization, complex integrations, troubleshooting)
  - Partner Community: Private forum/Slack for Q&A and peer support, best practices sharing, monthly "Partner Office Hours" with experts
  - Annual Partner Summit: In-person networking/training, roadmap previews, recognition/awards, certification advancement opportunities
  - Mandatory training attendance for certified partners, CPE (Continuing Professional Education) credit system

• **Partner Certification Advancement & Specialization**
  - Associate → Professional: After 2 successful supervised deliveries, PS Overlay review/approval, may require additional training
  - Professional → Expert: After 5 successful deliveries with ≥4.5 avg CSAT, demonstrated complex capability, community contribution, leadership interview/assessment
  - Specialization Tracks (Advanced): Industry (Healthcare, Financial Services, Public Sector, Manufacturing), Technical (Salesforce/SharePoint integrations, custom development, enterprise architecture)
  - Specialization benefits: Preferred allocation for matching opportunities, higher billing rates, marketing recognition, exclusive training access

• **Partner Performance Trending & Analytics**
  - Partner Performance Dashboard (for Program Team): Real-time per-partner metrics (active engagements, historical delivery, CSAT trailing 12mo, revenue contribution, Net New ARR, certification status, training completion, community engagement)
  - Program-level aggregates: Total capacity, utilization rate, quality trends, CSAT trends, attrition/retention
  - Predictive Analytics: Early warning indicators (engagement declining, CSAT trending down, late deliveries, low training participation)
  - Proactive management: Reach out to at-risk partners, provide intervention, adjust allocation to capability

• **Program Process Optimization**
  - Quarterly Program Review (Program Leadership): Review program performance (partner satisfaction, customer satisfaction, operational efficiency, financial performance)
  - Identify bottlenecks: Allocation delays, approval slowdowns, communication gaps, documentation burdens
  - Implement improvements: Streamline workflows, automate (allocation matching algorithm), update templates/tools, refine policies
  - Partner Feedback Surveys (Semi-Annual): Survey on program satisfaction, enablement quality, allocation fairness, communication, support, commercial terms, suggestions
  - Analyze feedback, implement improvements, communicate changes ("you said, we did")
  - Competitive Benchmarking: Monitor competitor partner programs (margins, enablement, incentives, allocation models)

• **Strategic Partner Expansion & Market Coverage**
  - Geographic Expansion: Assess coverage gaps (regions with demand but no partners, timezone coverage), targeted recruitment for gap regions
  - Capacity Planning: Forecast future demand (SPOP renewal pipeline, market adoption), calculate capacity needed, proactive recruitment to stay ahead
  - Balance: Enough partners for capacity, not so many that utilization suffers (target: ≥3 engagements per partner per year)
  - Strategic Partner Tier (Advanced): Criteria (consistent high performance ≥4.7 CSAT, significant ARR >$200K annually, Expert certification, strategic alignment, long-term commitment)
  - Strategic benefits: Priority allocation (first right of refusal), enhanced margin (+5-10%), joint marketing, executive relationships, quarterly business reviews, roadmap input, potential exclusivity in markets/industries

• **Program Innovation & Evolution**
  - New Service Offerings: Expand beyond K2 migration (ongoing support/optimization, CoE setup, advanced automation consulting, workflow optimization services)
  - Technology Enhancements: Migration accelerators (automated inventory/analysis tools, workflow templates, testing frameworks), Partner portal enhancements (self-service pipeline, automated reporting, resource library, collaboration tools)
  - Commercial Innovation: Subscription models (recurring revenue), outcome-based pricing (success fees), MSP arrangements (ongoing support), partner-led resale (channel evolution)

### Notes:
**Q: How do we keep partners engaged during slow periods (low opportunity flow)?**
A: Provide continuous value through training, community, early roadmap access, marketing support. Consider "bench time" initiatives (contribute to methodology, mentor others).

**Q: What if a partner stops performing after initial success?**
A: Investigate root cause (capacity overload, team changes, market distraction). Provide support and development plan. If no improvement, reduce allocation or exit from program.

**Q: How do we balance program standardization with partner autonomy?**
A: Core quality standards and processes are non-negotiable. Partners have flexibility in approach, tools, team structure as long as outcomes achieved.

**Q: Should we cap the number of certified partners?**
A: No hard cap, but manage total count to ensure adequate utilization (target: ≥3 engagements per partner per year). If utilization drops, pause recruitment.

**Q: How do we handle partner requests for exclusivity?**
A: Generally avoid exclusivity (limits customer choice and flexibility). Consider for Strategic Partners in limited scenarios where significant investment/commitment warranted.
